Ethical Considerations in IT

Developers and IT professionals must adhere to ethical guidelines to ensure responsible technology use.

Topics Covered

  • Technology and Society:
    • Balancing technological growth with respect for cultural and societal values.
    • Ensuring technology does not exacerbate social inequalities.
  • Case Studies:
    • Boeing Max: Accountability in engineering failures and prioritizing safety over profits.
    • eScooters: Addressing environmental impacts and equitable access to urban mobility solutions.
  • Privacy and Surveillance:
    • Balancing the need for surveillance with the right to personal privacy.
    • Preventing misuse of collected data for unauthorized purposes.
  • Misinformation and Disinformation:
    • Developing ethical frameworks to combat the spread of false information.
    • Promoting transparency and accountability in content creation and sharing.
  • Face Detection Technology:
    • Ensuring facial recognition algorithms are free from racial or gender bias.
    • Restricting unethical uses, such as unauthorized surveillance.
  • Deepfake AI:
    • Preventing malicious use of manipulated media for fraud or misinformation.
    • Establishing clear guidelines for ethical usage of deepfake technology.
  • Large Language Models (AI):
    • Addressing biases in AI-generated content and ensuring fairness in output.
    • Preventing misuse of AI for generating harmful or false information.
  • Food, Farms, and Technology:
    • Ensuring fair access to agricultural technology for small-scale farmers.
    • Minimizing environmental harm while enhancing food production efficiency.
  • Robotics and Ethics:
    • Addressing the displacement of workers by automation responsibly.
    • Ensuring human oversight in critical robotic applications.

Referenced from our class modules as well as the ACM - ACM Code of Ethics.
